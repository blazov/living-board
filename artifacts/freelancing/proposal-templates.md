# Upwork Proposal Templates

Five customizable templates for core service areas. Replace all `[bracketed]` fields before sending. Each template follows the same structure: personalized opener, scope, timeline, pricing, portfolio reference, and a closing question to invite a reply.

---

## General Proposal Tips

1. **Read the job post fully before writing.** Reference at least one specific detail from the post in your opener. Generic openers ("I am interested in your project") are deleted on sight.

2. **Lead with what you can do for them, not your background.** Save credentials for paragraph two. The first sentence should name a specific outcome the client wants.

3. **Be concrete about deliverables.** Vague language ("high-quality work") loses to specific language ("a 1,200-word SEO post targeting your primary keyword with meta description, H2 structure, and one internal link suggestion").

4. **Keep it under 200 words.** Clients scan proposals. A focused 150-word proposal outperforms a thorough 400-word one. If you need more space, use a table for deliverables.

5. **End with a question.** It creates a natural reason for the client to reply and signals you're paying attention to their specific situation, not blasting the same message to 50 people.

6. **Attach a relevant sample.** One sample that closely matches their request is worth more than a portfolio of ten unrelated pieces. If no exact match exists, attach the closest analog and note why it's relevant.

## Template 1 — Content Repurposing

> **Best for:** Clients posting about social media content, content strategy, turning blog posts or podcasts into social posts.

---

Subject: Repurposing [type of content] into [target platforms] — ready to start this week

Hi [Client Name],

You're looking for someone to turn your [blog posts / podcast episodes / newsletter] into content that actually gets used on [LinkedIn / Instagram / Twitter]. I've helped [type of client, e.g., "B2B SaaS founders"] do exactly that — taking one piece of long-form content and spinning it into a full week of social posts without the copy sounding like a summary or a press release.

**What I'd deliver:**
- [X] platform-optimized posts per [blog post / episode], with hooks written for each platform's format
- Hashtag sets and posting notes
- [Optional: carousel slide copy for LinkedIn or Instagram]

**Timeline:** [2–3] business days per batch

**Pricing:** $[XX] for [scope], or $[XX]/month for an ongoing retainer covering [X posts/week]

I've attached a sample — [describe sample briefly, e.g., "a LinkedIn repurposing set I did for a SaaS company's monthly roundup post"]. Happy to do a short sample from one of your existing posts if that's useful before you commit.

Quick question: are you prioritizing one platform right now, or trying to maintain presence across all of them?

[Your name]

## Template 2 — Data Research & Lead Generation

> **Best for:** Clients posting about lead lists, prospecting, market research, competitor analysis, or sales enablement.

---

Subject: Verified lead list for [niche/ICP] — no fillers, no guesswork

Hi [Client Name],

You need a clean lead list of [target ICP, e.g., "Series A SaaS founders in the HR tech space"], not a bulk export with 40% bounce rates. I build lists manually verified by LinkedIn and [Hunter / Apollo / direct site check], so every contact I hand over is someone you can actually reach.

**What I'd deliver:**
- [X] verified leads matching your ICP: [name, title, company, LinkedIn URL, email, notes field]
- Delivered as [Google Sheet / CSV] with source documentation
- [Optional: brief competitor overview — positioning, pricing, messaging — for [X] competitors]

**Timeline:** [2–4] business days depending on list size

**Pricing:** $[XX] for [X leads], or $[XX] for [larger volume] with the competitor analysis included

Sample attached — [describe: e.g., "a 50-lead list I built for a fintech agency targeting VP-level buyers, including the verification methodology I used"]. You can review format and coverage before deciding.

One question before I start: is there a specific geographic region or company size range you want to filter for?

[Your name]
