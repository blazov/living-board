**Turn one piece of content into a full week of social media posts — without writing a word.**

You spent hours writing that blog post. Now it's sitting there doing nothing while your competitors are everywhere. I'll take your existing long-form content and transform it into 5 high-performing social media formats, each tailored to the platform's native style and audience behavior.

**What I do:**

I don't just copy-paste sentences and call it "repurposing." I re-engineer your content for each platform's format, attention span, and culture. A LinkedIn carousel reads nothing like a Twitter/X thread, and an Instagram caption is a completely different animal than a Facebook post.

**The 5 formats I deliver (Standard and Premium):**

- **Twitter/X Thread** — A numbered thread with a scroll-stopping opener, punchy insights, and a closer that drives profile visits or link clicks
- **Instagram Caption** — A hook, body story, and hashtag block optimized for reach (up to 30 relevant hashtags included)
- **Facebook Post** — Conversational, community-oriented copy tuned for the algorithm's preference for engagement triggers
- **Email Newsletter Snippet** — A 150-200 word teaser that drives clicks back to the original post, written for 40%+ open-rate subject lines

**Why work with me?**

- I write in YOUR voice — I study your existing content before writing a single word
- Every format respects platform-native conventions; your LinkedIn followers won't feel like they're reading an Instagram ad
- Keyword and hashtag research is included — not an afterthought
- Delivered with a brief "How to Post" note for each format so you or your VA can publish without guessing

**Who this is for:** Content creators, SaaS founders, coaches, agencies, bloggers, and marketing teams who have good content but not enough hours to distribute it everywhere.

**Not sure which package fits?** Message me first — I'll tell you honestly.

## Launch Sequence Strategy

_Follow this sequence when activating new gigs on Fiverr for maximum algorithm traction._

### Week 1: Pre-Launch Preparation (before going live)

**Profile foundation first.** The gig algorithm rewards sellers with complete, consistent profiles. Before activating any gig:

- Professional photo (headshot, plain background, direct eye contact)
- Headline that uses primary keywords from your top gig (e.g., "Technical Writer | API Docs | SaaS Documentation")
- Bio of 150-200 words that mentions your three service areas naturally
- Skills section: fill all available slots with relevant terms
- Set your "online" hours to align with US Eastern or UK time (highest buyer volume)

**Create all three gigs in draft first.** Do not publish until all three are ready simultaneously. Launching together signals an active, committed seller to the algorithm.

**Prepare your gig images.** Fiverr gig thumbnails have outsized click-through impact. Use Canva. Each thumbnail needs:
- A bold, readable headline (7 words max)
- A contrasting color scheme (avoid all-white or all-black)
- A visual cue for the service type (document icon for tech writing, social icons for repurposing, etc.)
- Your face or a professional stock image — human images outperform graphics

**Set up your FAQ responses in advance.** The first 5-7 buyer questions you receive will likely be similar. Draft polished, brief replies you can send within minutes of receiving them.

### Week 2: Launch Day and First 72 Hours

**Publish all three gigs on a Tuesday or Wednesday** — these days have the highest Fiverr buyer traffic.

**Send your first 10 buyer requests within 24 hours.** Fiverr's "Buyer Requests" board (or the newer Briefs section) surfaces active buyers. Respond to every relevant request with a customized, 3-4 sentence pitch. Do not use templates — personalization is detectable and rewarded.

**Target your first order aggressively.** The algorithm heavily weights new gigs that get early orders and reviews. Consider these tactics:
- Offer a small discount to your first 3 buyers (mention it in the gig description temporarily)
- Reach out to anyone in your existing network who could legitimately use the service
- Post about the gig launch on LinkedIn, Twitter/X, or relevant Slack communities

**Respond to every message within 1 hour for the first week.** Response rate and response time are direct algorithm inputs. A 1-hour average response time puts you in the top tier.

### Weeks 3-4: Gaining Traction

**Deliver early orders faster than promised.** If your turnaround is 3 days, deliver in 2. Early 5-star reviews with comments like "delivered ahead of schedule" are algorithmic gold.

**Ask for reviews tactfully.** When delivering an order, include a brief closing line: "If you're happy with the work, a short review helps others find me — I'd really appreciate it." Do not offer incentives for reviews; that violates Fiverr TOS.

**Optimize based on click data.** After 2 weeks, check your gig analytics. If impressions are high but clicks are low, your thumbnail is the problem. If clicks are high but orders are low, your pricing or description needs work.

**Begin stacking reviews across all three gigs.** Fiverr shows sellers differently once they hit 10, 25, and 50 reviews. Push all three gigs toward 10 reviews before Month 2.

## Algorithm Optimization Tips

_Ongoing tactics to maintain and improve gig ranking._

### Title and Tag Optimization

- **Titles must front-load the primary keyword.** Fiverr's search algorithm weights the first 3-4 words of your title most heavily. "I will repurpose your blog post" puts the primary keyword ("repurpose blog post") near the front.
- **Use all 5 tag slots.** Research tags by searching Fiverr for your service and noting autocomplete suggestions — these reflect actual buyer search volume.
- **Tags should cover variations.** Mix high-volume broad tags ("content repurposing") with specific long-tail tags ("blog to Twitter thread") to capture different search intents.
- **Update tags seasonally.** Buyer search behavior shifts. Revisit your tags every 90 days.

### Pricing and Conversion

- **Basic package must be under $35 for impulse purchases.** The $25-30 range captures buyers who are "testing" a new seller.
- **The Standard package is your volume driver.** Price it at roughly 2x Basic and make the value jump obvious. For most gigs, Standard delivers 60-70% of revenue.
- **Premium packages build perceived authority.** Even if few buyers purchase it, a well-structured Premium package makes Standard look like a bargain by comparison.
- **Enable "Custom Offers."** Many high-value buyers will message before ordering. A prompt, professional custom offer converts these inquiries at a high rate.

### Gig Description SEO

- **First 150 characters are indexed most heavily.** Start every description with a benefit-oriented sentence that contains your primary keyword naturally.
- **Use your top 3 keywords 3-4 times each** throughout the description, but only where they read naturally.
- **Bold key phrases** using Fiverr's text editor. This aids scannability and may carry minor SEO weight.
- **Keep paragraphs short.** No wall of text. Buyers scan, not read. Short paragraphs, bullets, and headers reduce bounce.

### Maintaining Rank

- **Stay active daily.** Log in every day, even if briefly. Activity signals keep gigs from being deprioritized.
- **Refresh gig descriptions every 60-90 days.** Small edits signal to the algorithm that your gig is maintained and current.
- **Never cancel orders if avoidable.** Cancellation rate is one of the most punishing ranking factors. If an order goes wrong, attempt a resolution before cancelling — even offering a partial refund to avoid cancellation is often worth it.
- **Achieve and maintain "Seller Level" thresholds.** Level 1 (10 orders + 4.7 stars in 60 days), Level 2, and Top Rated Seller each unlock better algorithm positioning, Pro badge eligibility, and buyer trust signals.
- **Respond to every review.** Replying to reviews — especially positive ones — keeps your profile active and shows buyers you're engaged.

### Cross-Gig Strategy

These three gigs are designed to cross-sell naturally:

- A buyer who orders Content Repurposing may need lead generation help to distribute that content to prospects — upsell to Gig 2.
- A buyer who orders B2B lead lists for a SaaS company almost certainly needs better product documentation to convert those leads — upsell to Gig 3.
- A buyer who orders API Documentation may want their release notes or changelogs repurposed as social content — upsell to Gig 1.

In each order delivery message, include one natural sentence pointing to a related gig: "If you're looking to [related need], I also offer [gig name] — happy to put together a custom bundle."
